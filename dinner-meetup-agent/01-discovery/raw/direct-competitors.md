## Competitive Landscape Summary

### Market Concentration

The Chinese dining decision space is **oligopolistic at the platform level** (Meituan/Dianping/ByteDance dominate) but **fragmented at the "group coordination" level** where no clear leader exists.

```
Platform Layer (concentrated):
  Meituan/Dianping ─── ~65% food delivery, dominant in-store
  ByteDance/Douyin ─── Rapidly growing local life (~9000亿 GMV)
  
Decision Layer (fragmented, manual):
  WeChat tools ─── 180M+ users but generic, no AI
  Randomizers ─── Niche, individual only
  Nothing ─── (most common "solution")
  
AI Layer (emerging, individual-focused):
  探饭 (ByteDance) ─── 35K users, individual
  小美 (Meituan) ─── Beta, individual
```

### Key Gaps and Positioning Opportunities

1. **No group preference fusion exists.** Every competitor focuses on individual recommendations. No product collects multiple people's preferences and synthesizes them into one plan.

2. **The "操心人" (organizer) is unserved.** Current tools help individuals find restaurants, but nobody helps the person who has to coordinate 4-6 people's conflicting preferences.

3. **AI + social coordination whitespace.** ByteDance and Meituan are racing to add AI to individual recommendations. Neither has touched the multi-person coordination problem.

4. **WeChat-native opportunity.** WeChat ecosystem tools (接龙, 投票) have 180M+ users but no AI layer. Building a WeChat-mini-program-native solution with AI intelligence could bridge the gap.

5. **"Full plan" vs "single restaurant."** All existing tools recommend restaurants. None create a complete plan (restaurant + activity + timing). This is an open differentiation space.

### Competitive Dynamics

- **Meituan vs ByteDance:** The two giants are locked in a "内容生态与商业效率两大体系的终极对决" (clash between content ecosystem and business efficiency). Both are adding AI, but to individual discovery, not group coordination.
- **AI democratization:** LLM costs are dropping rapidly. Both ByteDance (Doubao) and Meituan (LongCat) have in-house models. Our differentiation must be the coordination workflow, not the AI recommendation itself.
- **微信 ecosystem lock-in:** Any new dining tool must work within WeChat. Both Meituan and ByteDance have WeChat mini-programs. Our H5 approach is correct but distribution is the challenge.
- **No venture-backed pure-play competitor:** No startup was found that has raised venture funding specifically for AI-powered group dining coordination. This is both opportunity (whitespace) and risk (why hasn't anyone done it?).

### Positioning Recommendation

**"今天整点啥" should position as: the AI coordinator, not the AI recommender.**

- Meituan/Dianping/ByteDance recommend restaurants to individuals
- "今天整点啥" coordinates preferences across groups
- The differentiation is the **multi-agent coordination workflow**, not the restaurant database or recommendation algorithm

## Red Flags

1. **No venture-backed startup competitor found.** This could mean the market is wide open (opportunity) or that smart money has determined this space is unviable (risk). Need to understand why. [Data Gap]
2. **Meituan and ByteDance can add group features quickly.** Their AI investments are individual-focused today but could pivot. The coordination workflow is the only moat, and it is thin. [Opinion]
3. **WeChat ecosystem is both channel and cage.** All distribution must go through WeChat. WeChat policy changes could kill the product overnight. [Risk]
4. **Low-frequency use case.** Monthly 2-4 times dining frequency makes retention inherently difficult. No competitor has solved this. [Data]
5. **Network effects are weak.** Group dining coordination has some viral potential (one person invites friends) but no strong retention loop after the meal. [Opinion]

## Yellow Flags

1. **TanFan's 35K users in 2 weeks is impressive distribution speed.** ByteDance can push products to millions quickly. [Data]
2. **"吃什么" randomizer tools exist but are tiny.** Validates the "indecision pain" exists but suggests pure decision tools without content/booking have limited appeal. [Observation]
3. **Yami's invite-only, ad-free model may be unsustainable.** Questions whether "pure" recommendation without monetization can survive within profit-focused Meituan. [Opinion]
4. **Douyin's 9000亿 GMV milestone.** Indicates ByteDance is serious about local services and will invest heavily. [Data]
5. **International swipe-for-restaurants apps (MunchMatch, GroupEats) exist but haven't scaled.** Suggests the group dining decision space is hard to crack. [Observation]
